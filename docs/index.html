How the simulation works

The scenario begins in an indoor public environment when a virtual character collapses due to sudden cardiac arrest. The AI tutor guides the user through each phase of the official BLSD protocol.

01

Safety & Assessment

Visual inspection of the area and assessment of the victim's responsiveness and breathing.

02

Call 112 (Emergency)

Alerting emergency services via the interactive phone integrated into the simulation.

03

CPR

Chest compressions and artificial respiration to maintain blood circulation.
Read about future implementation ↗

04

AED Defibrillation

Locating and using the public AED with step-by-step visual and vocal guidance.

Technical Details

Project Insights

The technical and design innovations that make VR-BLSD a unique experience.

🤖

Conversational AI Tutor — ConvAI

Integration of an adaptive voice AI for real-time procedural feedback and management of the user's emotional response during simulated panic scenarios.

🎙️ Custom Push-to-Talk

Custom voice activation system integrated directly into Unreal Engine with Blueprints, optimized for minimum latency in standalone VR mode.

↩️ Dynamic Character Look-At

The MetaHuman chatbot autonomously rotates towards the user during dialogue, creating natural eye contact that increases presence and the realism of the conversation.

⚡ Proactive Dialogue — AI-First

Unlike traditional chatbots, the ConvAI tutor autonomously initiates the first interaction as soon as the user enters the scene, lowering the barrier to entry and making the experience more natural and cinematic.

📍 Autonomous Pathfinding & Spatial Awareness

Leveraging Unreal Engine's navigation system, the ConvAI agent autonomously tracks and moves toward the MetaHuman's position in real-time. This ensures seamless spatial interaction, allowing the chatbot to proactively reach the user's location based on dynamic gameplay events.

📞

Interactive Phone — Calling 112

A fully functional in-world phone with a phonebook, audio, and differentiated handling between Desktop and VR modes.

📱 Dual-Mode: Desktop & VR

In Desktop mode, the user interacts via mouse and keyboard with a classic on-screen interface. In VR mode, interaction is physical: the user grabs the phone, scrolls through the phonebook with their hands, and lifts the receiver to dial the emergency number — realistic audio included.

⚡

AED Objects — Interactive Defibrillator

Complete interaction system with the defibrillator: from opening the AED to attaching the pads to the victim.

🔓 Opening AED & Pads

The AED can be physically opened in the VR environment. Inside, the electrode pads are visible and grabbable; they can be dragged and placed correctly on the victim's chest following the tutor's vocal instructions.

🗄️ AED Holder with Animation

To increase environmental realism, a wall mount for the AED (AED Holder) was designed. The user can interact with it to open and close it via a smooth animation, revealing the defibrillator inside — exactly as would happen in a real emergency.

Roadmap

Future Improvements

While the current prototype successfully demonstrates the viability of an AI-guided BLSD simulation, several avenues remain for expanding its educational scope and realism, ensuring strict alignment with the procedural steps outlined in the international resuscitation guidelines.

🚑

Enhanced EMS Interaction

Currently, the simulation simplifies the process of alerting emergency medical services. A future iteration will implement a dynamic conversation tree with the dispatcher, requiring the user to accurately answer standard assessment questions (e.g., patient responsiveness, breathing status, exact location) in accordance with official BLSD protocols.

🫀

CPR Mechanics

Although the prototype focuses on early defibrillation, manual chest compressions and rescue breaths are vital links in the chain of survival. Future versions will introduce interactive CPR mechanics, challenging users to maintain the correct compression depth and tempo under pressure.

⚡

AED Shock Delivery

The final phase of the Automated External Defibrillator protocol will be fully implemented. This will require the user to actively ensure the safety of the scene before manually confirming and delivering the simulated shock.
